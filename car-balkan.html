Balkan
Self-Drive Car Tour

9 Days / 8 Nights ~2,000 km BMW X3 / Renault Captur Convoy Tour

Tour Overview

The Balkans offer one of Europe's most dramatic and underexplored road trip routes. In just nine days, you'll drive through four countries — Croatia, Bosnia & Herzegovina, Montenegro, and back through Croatia — threading together UNESCO World Heritage sites, Ottoman bazaars, Adriatic coastline, and Byzantine sea fortresses. All in the comfort of a BMW X3 or Renault Captur, with full MotoRover support throughout.

This is not a highway tour. Every route segment has been chosen for its scenery, cultural significance, or pure driving pleasure. The serpentine descent into Kotor Bay, the coastal Makarska Riviera, the mountain road into Sarajevo — these are roads that stay with you long after you've returned home.

Tour Highlights

UNESCO Kotor Bay — one of Europe's most dramatic sea inlets
Stari Most, Mostar — the iconic 16th-century Ottoman bridge
UNESCO Dubrovnik — the pearl of the Adriatic
Plitvice Lakes — turquoise cascading waterfalls in deep forest
Diocletian's Palace, Split — living Roman monument (UNESCO)
Sutjeska National Park — primeval forest & Balkan canyon
4 countries in 9 days — Croatia, Bosnia, Montenegro
Full convoy support: tour manager, mechanic, GPS

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day Date Route Activity Distance
Day 1 Apr 10 Zagreb Arrival, Gornji Grad old city, St. Mark's Church, welcome dinner — km
Day 2 Apr 11 Zagreb → Plitvice → Split UNESCO Plitvice Lakes — turquoise terraced waterfalls in ancient forest. Board the cars and press south through Dalmatia to Split, Croatia's storied coastal city. 340 km
Day 3 Apr 12 Split Diocletian's Palace (UNESCO) — a Roman emperor's retirement home now lived in and loved by an entire city. Day trip to medieval Trogir. Optional Brač island ferry. — km
Day 4 Apr 13 Split → Makarska → Mostar Drive the Adriatic Riviera — limestone karst cliffs dropping into impossibly blue sea. Cross the Neretva into Bosnia. Stari Most: the 16th-century Ottoman bridge that defines Mostar, rebuilt after war, now a UNESCO symbol of reconciliation. 130 km
Day 5 Apr 14 Mostar → Sarajevo The mountain road to Sarajevo is one of Europe's most underrated drives — through river gorges and cedar forests. The city itself is the Olympic city of 1984: Baščaršija bazaar, the war memorial, the intersection of empires. 130 km
Day 6 Apr 15 Sarajevo → Montenegro Drive south through Sutjeska National Park — a UNESCO-protected primeval forest and the site of some of Europe's deepest river canyons. The road is breathtaking. Montenegro border crossing and arrival in the highlands. 200 km
Day 7 Apr 16 Montenegro → Kotor The serpentine descent from the Montenegrin plateau to Kotor Bay is one of the finest drives in Europe — 25 hairpin bends with the sea opening below you at every turn. UNESCO Kotor's medieval old city awaits at the bottom. 150 km
Day 8 Apr 17 Kotor → Budva → Dubrovnik Drive the Montenegrin Riviera — past the island village of Sveti Stefan, one of the most photographed scenes in the Adriatic. Cross into Croatia and arrive in Dubrovnik, the pearl of the coast, for a farewell dinner. 160 km
Day 9 Apr 18 Dubrovnik Walk the full circuit of Dubrovnik's Old City walls — one of the great walks in Europe. Fort Lovrijenac in the morning light. Departure. The Balkans do not let go easily. — km
