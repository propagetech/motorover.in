Car Tour Morocco Apr 28 – May 8, 2026

Morocco
Self-Drive

11 Days / 10 Nights ~2,400 km Toyota Land Cruiser Prado Casablanca Loop

Tour Overview

Morocco is an assault on the senses — in the best possible way. From the blue-washed alleys of Chefchaouen to the infinite silence of the Sahara at Merzouga, from the medieval labyrinth of Fès to the wind-battered Atlantic coast at Essaouira, this 11-day self-drive is the most complete Morocco journey you can make behind a steering wheel.

You'll drive a Toyota Land Cruiser Prado or Renault Duster through Sahara desert tracks, over the High Atlas passes, and along the Draa Valley's ancient ksar road — through four UNESCO World Heritage cities and into the deep desert. Nights alternate between ornate riads in imperial cities and a luxury desert camp beneath a star-filled Saharan sky.

Tour Highlights

Merzouga Erg Chebbi — camel trek into the Sahara and a night at a luxury desert camp
UNESCO Ait Benhaddou — the most-filmed ksar on earth, rising from the Draa plain
Chefchaouen — the blue pearl of the Rif Mountains, one of Morocco's most iconic towns
UNESCO Fès el-Bali — the world's largest car-free medina and a living medieval city
Marrakech Djemaa el-Fna — the great square at dusk, alive with performers and food stalls
Draa Valley drive — a 200 km corridor of kasbahs, palm oases, and rose-tinted gorges
Desert camp night — 4WD dune driving, camel ride, stargazing, Berber music
Tizi n'Tichka High Atlas pass — the most dramatic mountain road in North Africa

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day Date Route Activity Distance
Day 1 Apr 28 Casablanca Arrival. Visit the magnificent Hassan II Mosque — the third-largest in the world, perched on the Atlantic. Welcome dinner in the city. — km
Day 2 Apr 29 Casablanca → Rabat → Chefchaouen Pass through UNESCO-listed Rabat, Morocco's graceful capital. Arrive in the legendary blue city of Chefchaouen, draped across the Rif Mountains — every alley a photograph. 340 km
Day 3 Apr 30 Chefchaouen → Fès Drive south through the Rif foothills to Fès — Morocco's spiritual and intellectual capital. Check in to a riad inside the medina. Evening wander through the lamplit souks. 200 km
Day 4 May 1 Fès — Full Day Full day in the medina. Chouara tanneries with their vivid dye pits, the ornate Bou Inania Madrasa, spice souks, the copper quarter. Afternoon hammam experience. Dinner in a traditional Fassi palace. — km
Day 5 May 2 Fès → Ifrane → Azrou → Midelt Cross the Middle Atlas — a landscape of cedar forests, alpine meadows, and Barbary macaques. Ifrane, Morocco's Swiss village. Drive through to the gateway of the desert at Midelt. 280 km
Day 6 May 3 Midelt → Merzouga The desert beckons. Drive south across the Ziz Gorge and through the Tafilalet oases to Merzouga, where the Erg Chebbi dunes rise 150 metres from the flat hammada. Check in to a luxury desert camp at the dune base. 300 km
Day 7 May 4 Merzouga Full Sahara day. Dawn camel trek into the dunes, sunrise from the top. 4WD sand driving across the erg. Afternoon rest in the tent. Sunset on the dunes with Berber tea. Night: traditional music around the fire under a sky blazing with stars. — km
Day 8 May 5 Merzouga → Draa Valley → Zagora Drive the magnificent Draa Valley — a 150 km corridor of kasbahs, rose fields, and palm-fringed oases. Pass through Agdez and Tamnougalt kasbahs. Arrive in Zagora as the palm groves glow in the late afternoon. 280 km
Day 9 May 6 Zagora → Ouarzazate → Ait Benhaddou Drive to Ouarzazate — Morocco's Hollywood. Visit the UNESCO ksar of Ait Benhaddou, filming location for Lawrence of Arabia, Gladiator, and Game of Thrones. A crumbling earthen citadel of extraordinary beauty. 180 km
Day 10 May 7 Ouarzazate → Marrakech → Essaouira Cross the High Atlas via the Tizi n'Tichka pass — Morocco's most dramatic mountain road at 2,260m. Descend to Marrakech for a medina lunch. Continue to the windswept Atlantic port of Essaouira for a farewell dinner by the sea. 360 km
Day 11 May 8 Essaouira → Casablanca Final coastal drive north along the Atlantic Highway. Vehicle return, airport transfers, departure. Morocco leaves its mark on every traveller who drives it. 360 km
