Northern Europe
Self-Drive Car Tour

9 Days / 8 Nights ~1,800 km Toyota RAV4 / Audi Q8 Convoy Tour

Tour Overview

The Baltic states are Europe's most underrated road trip. Three countries — Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania — packed into nine days of medieval walled cities, Art Nouveau masterpieces, Baroque palaces, ancient coastal dunes, and primeval forests. This is Northern Europe at its most authentic and unhurried. In a Toyota RAV4 or Audi Q8, you have the comfort to truly absorb it.

The route begins in UNESCO Tallinn — arguably Europe's best-preserved medieval city — and ends in UNESCO Vilnius, one of Baroque architecture's finest examples. Between them: Riga's Art Nouveau district (a UNESCO World Heritage concentration unique on earth), Latvia's Gauja National Park gorges, the Baroque Rundale Palace (often called "Latvia's Versailles"), and Lithuania's haunting Hill of Crosses pilgrimage site — 200,000 crosses placed one by one across a sacred hillside. This is a tour that surprises everyone who takes it.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day Date Route Activity Meals Distance
Day 1Aug TBATallinnArrival. UNESCO Old Town walk: Fat Margaret tower, Toompea Hill fortress, the Town Hall square, St. Olaf's Church. Welcome dinner in a cellar restaurant in the medieval lower city. Meals included — Dinner — km
Day 2—Tallinn → PärnuDrive south to Pärnu — Estonia's most beloved spa resort town. Wooden Art Nouveau resort villas line leafy boulevards behind a fine sandy beach. The unhurried Baltic summer at its most refined. Meals included — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner 130 km
Day 3—Pärnu → Cape Kolka → JūrmalaDrive the Latvian coast to Cape Kolka — the wild, windswept tip where the Gulf of Riga meets the open Baltic. Ancient fishermen's villages and coastal wetland reserves. Continue to Jūrmala, the great Soviet-era Baltic beach resort, still vivid and surprising. Meals included — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner 210 km
Day 4—Jūrmala → RigaA short drive into Latvia's capital — but Riga rewards days of exploration. The UNESCO Art Nouveau district is unlike anything else in Europe: entire streets of elaborate floral and figurative facades from the 1900s. Central Market (the largest in Europe), the Blackheads House, the medieval old city, the Latvian National Opera. Meals included — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner 50 km
Day 5—Riga (full day)Full day in the city. Riga Cathedral with its medieval cloister, the Latvian National Museum of Art, the extraordinary Riga Art Nouveau Museum in an untouched original apartment. Evening: rooftop dinner over the old city spires. Meals included — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner — km
Day 6—Riga → Sigulda → CēsisDrive into the Gauja National Park — Latvia's green heart. Turaida Castle overlooking a deep river gorge, the medieval ruins of Sigulda Castle, the bobsled track built for Soviet athletes. Continue to Cēsis — a beautiful medieval Hanseatic town that feels undiscovered by the world. Meals included — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner 120 km
Day 7—Cēsis → Rundale → Lithuanian borderVisit Rundale Palace — Bartolomeo Rastrelli's 1736 Baroque masterpiece, the same architect who built the Winter Palace in St. Petersburg. The Golden Hall and White Hall are among the finest interiors in Northern Europe. Cross the rolling Latvian farmland into Lithuania. Meals included — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner 160 km
Day 8—Border → Kaunas → Hill of CrossesKaunas — Lithuania's second city and cultural heart: the IX Fort war museum, the old town castle, the Žaliakalnis Art Deco quarter. Then north to Šiauliai and the Hill of Crosses — a sacred pilgrimage hill covered in over 200,000 crosses, rosaries, and carvings. An image that never leaves you. Meals included — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner 280 km
Day 9—VilniusDrive to the Baroque capital. Gediminas Tower on its hill above the city, the Cathedral Square, Bernardine Gardens, the bohemian Užupis quarter (its own micro-republic with its own constitution). Farewell dinner in a courtyard restaurant in the old city. The Baltics do not release their travellers willingly. Meals included — Breakfast 100 km
